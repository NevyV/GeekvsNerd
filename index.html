"You're a geek!" "You're a nerd!" Is that a compliment, a diss, or what? What does that even mean, anyway? It can be confusing, especially since there's some crossover between the two, making possible geeky nerds…or nerdy geeks! Geeks and Nerds tipically get placed as the same types of people. However, there are many differences between the two.

Geek

An enthusiast of a particular topic or field. Geeks are generally very knowledgeable—even to the point of obsession—about a particular topic.

It's important to note that geeks are generally social. They have their fascination in what makes them unique, but you probably wouldn't know of their geekitude unless they told you about it.

Nerd

A studious intellectual, although again of a particular topic or field. Nerds are “achievement” oriented, and focus their efforts on acquiring knowledge and skill over trivia and memorabilia.

The general connotation is of an irritating, unattractive person who may be brilliant, but chooses to focus on non-social pursuits.
